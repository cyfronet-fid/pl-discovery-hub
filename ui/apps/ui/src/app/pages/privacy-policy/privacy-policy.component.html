Privacy Policy of EOSC.pl Platform

Last updated: July 2025

The EOSC.pl Platform, available at https://eosc.pl/ (hereinafter referred to as "Site" or "Application") comprises two main functionalities:

  • one for users who browse and access research services and resources, and
  • one for organisations and providers who register and onboard their services (e.g., computing services, repositories, training tools).

This Privacy Notice outlines how personal data is collected, used, and protected across both functionalities, in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and related EU law.

We follow these key principles:
  • We collect only the minimum necessary personal data
  • We limit sharing to what is legally or operationally necessary
  • We provide clear options regarding data use
  • We protect your data with appropriate security measures

This Privacy Notice describes the policies applied to the personal information collected through the platform website, or when the platform communicates with its users ("you" or "user"), and the choices available to you. "Personal Information" means any data that relates to an identified or identifiable person, under Article 4(1) GDPR.

The platform offers a search and discovery feature that allows users to browse scientific services and digital resources, such as publications, datasets, software, tools, and training materials. Users are provided with a personalised search interface, including filters and categories tailored to improve relevance and user experience.

The platform also enables organisations and service providers to register and onboard their resources, making them discoverable and accessible to end users through the catalogue.

3. Personal data we collect

Legal basis for processing:

We process personal data under the following legal bases, as defined in Article 6 of the GDPR:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)), for providing access to platform features and services
  • Compliance with legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)), particularly for EU-funded project reporting
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)), for platform security, usage analytics, and user experience improvements
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)), where required for specific optional processing (e.g., cookies, newsletter)

The data we collect

The EOSC.pl Platform manages different types of personal data, all in compliance with applicable European data protection laws. Personal data concerning the user is collected and processed to enable the delivery of services and functionalities offered via the platform. This includes features aimed at:

  • enabling users to browse and access scientific services and resources, and
  • allowing provider organisations to register and onboard services into the platform.

The data is collected for the following purposes: service access, user profile management, analytics, support communication, tag and metadata management, displaying external content, and system security.

Depending on your interaction with the platform (either as a user or a service provider), the following types of personal data may be collected and processed by the platform, either directly or via third-party integrations:

  • Cookies and usage data
  • First name, last name
  • Email address
  • Affiliation/organisation name
  • Country of origin
  • Federated login attributes, such as your name, email address, organisation affiliation, and a unique user ID used for account recognition
  • User typology (e.g. researcher, citizen, provider, educator)
  • Reason for accessing EOSC services (if submitted voluntarily)
  • Information on accessed or ordered services
  • Messages and communication with the support team

Order Management functionality

For users who interact with the service ordering workflow, additional personal data may be processed, including:

  • Details of the service order, such as descriptions, configuration parameters, or project references
  • User identifiers and roles within the order flow
  • Organisation and project affiliations
  • Technical contact details and correspondence related to service requests
  • Messages exchanged between users and providers as part of order handling and support

This data is collected as part of delivering requested services, tracking progress, and enabling communication between involved parties.

Data voluntarily supplied by the user

The platform is designed to allow for anonymous browsing in most areas. However, access to personalised features (e.g., service ordering or provider registration) requires login and, in some cases, completion of a user profile.

In such cases, the following data may be voluntarily submitted:

  • Contact details (name, email, institution)
  • Service metadata (for providers)
  • Descriptions of use cases or projects

Providing this data is optional, but necessary to access specific features. Consent may be withdrawn at any time, although this may disable the corresponding functionality.

Service and resource metadata

When providers onboard the resources and services, the platform stores and processes service descriptions and metadata, structured according to a standardised schema. This includes:

  • Basic information: resource ID, name, description, provider identity
  • Marketing materials: target users, promotional texts, multimedia assets
  • Classification: scientific domains, categories
  • Geographic and language availability
  • Public contact details for user support
  • Maturity levels (e.g., TRL, release version, lifecycle phase)
  • Relations to other resources within EOSC
  • Project and funding references
  • Operational information: terms of use, helpdesk links, support SLA
  • Access, ordering, and usage constraints
  • Financial model or pricing details (if applicable)

This metadata is published to the platform's catalogue and may be publicly accessible, except for specific operational fields reserved for internal management.

Platform usage analytics

The platform collects aggregated data on how users interact with services and resources. This includes:

  • Anonymised or pseudonymised usage events (e.g., page views, searches, downloads)
  • Search terms and selected filters
  • Browsing history across sessions (if logged in)
  • Resource engagement statistics (e.g., views, clicks, interactions)

This data is used to:

  • Provide personalised recommendations (where applicable)
  • Generate aggregated statistics for platform improvement
  • Improve resource discoverability

No sensitive data or behavioural profiling is performed beyond what is required to improve relevance and user experience. Users may object to this processing.

Cookies and tracking technologies

Cookies and similar tracking technologies are used for:

  • Session management and login authentication
  • Remembering user preferences
  • Usage analytics
  • Displaying content from embedded third-party platforms (e.g., video, charts)

We use cookies and third-party tools to support core features and improve your experience. Where required by law, we ask for your consent.

Data retention

We retain personal data only as long as needed for platform operation, or as required by legal obligations or project agreements (e.g., retention for 3 years after project completion). The User can always request that the Data Controller suspend or remove the data.

Third-party data

Users are responsible for any third-party personal data that they provide (e.g., in uploaded service descriptions or messages) and confirm that they have the right to do so. The platform assumes that such data has been shared lawfully.

8. Your data protection rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) - You may request access to the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) - You can request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) - You can ask us to delete your data, unless legal grounds prevent us
  • Right to restrict processing (Art. 18) - You may request temporary suspension of processing
  • Right to object (Art. 21) - You may object to processing based on our legitimate interests
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) - You have the right to receive your data in a portable format, where applicable

Requests can be submitted via: ymlpd@cyfronet.pl

You also have the right to complain to a supervisory authority, such as the Polish Data Protection Office.

9. Security

We apply technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from loss, misuse, unauthorised access, alteration, or destruction. These measures are consistent with industry standards and include data access controls, secure infrastructure, and regular security assessments.

However, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee the security of:

  • our servers at all times

If you contact us by email, please note that messages are usually transmitted as plain text and may be vulnerable to interception. If your message includes sensitive or confidential information, we recommend contacting us first to arrange a secure communication method.
